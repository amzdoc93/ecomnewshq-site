What Is a Section 3 Inauthentic Suspension?

A Section 3 inauthentic suspension is one of the most severe actions Amazon takes against sellers. Unlike listing-level complaints, this is an account-level deactivation under Section 3 of the Amazon Business Solutions Agreement.

Amazon's notice typically states: "Your Amazon seller account has been deactivated in accordance with Section 3 of Amazon's Business Solutions Agreement. We took this action because we have concerns about the authenticity of items previously offered by you."

Why Section 3 Is Different From Standard Inauthentic Complaints

Standard inauthentic complaints affect individual ASINs. Section 3 suspensions affect your entire account — all listings, all funds, all inventory. The appeal process is more rigorous, the documentation requirements are stricter, and the consequence of failed appeals is more severe. Many sellers make the mistake of submitting the same type of appeal they'd use for a listing-level issue, which almost always fails.

Common Triggers for Section 3 Inauthentic Suspensions

  • Multiple inauthentic item complaints from different customers or brands
  • Insufficient or invalid supply chain documentation when requested by Amazon
  • Sourcing from unauthorized distributors or retail stores (retail arbitrage with no invoices)
  • High return rates with "inauthentic" reason codes from customers
  • Failed product authenticity tests by Amazon or brand test-buy programs

What Makes a Successful Section 3 POA

After resolving hundreds of Section 3 cases, we've identified the elements that separate successful appeals from rejected ones:

Must-Have Elements

  • ASIN-specific root cause analysis — address each flagged ASIN individually
  • Valid invoices — from verifiable suppliers, within 365 days, matching sales volume
  • Concrete corrective actions — not generic promises, but specific changes already implemented
  • Preventive systems — documented quality control, supplier vetting, and authenticity verification processes
  • Professional tone — factual, specific, and solution-focused (no emotional pleas)

Common Mistakes That Get Appeals Rejected

  • Using template POAs found online
  • Submitting retail receipts instead of wholesale invoices
  • Blaming Amazon, customers, or competitors
  • Being vague about corrective actions ("we will improve quality")
  • Submitting multiple weak appeals in rapid succession

Frequently Asked Questions

What is a Section 3 inauthentic suspension?
It's an account-level deactivation under Section 3 of Amazon's Business Solutions Agreement, triggered by authenticity concerns about products you've sold. It affects your entire account, not just individual listings.
How do I appeal a Section 3 suspension?
Submit a detailed Plan of Action through Seller Central addressing: root cause (what happened), corrective actions (what you fixed), and preventive measures (how you'll prevent it). Include valid invoices from authorized suppliers for all flagged ASINs.
How long does a Section 3 appeal take?
Typically 14-45 days. Initial response from Amazon comes in 5-7 days. Complex cases with multiple flagged ASINs can take 60+ days. We focus on first-appeal success to minimize the timeline.
What invoices does Amazon accept?
Amazon requires invoices within the last 365 days, from verifiable suppliers with physical addresses, showing quantities matching your sales, with itemized product names. Retail receipts are not accepted.
Can I get reinstated after Section 3?
Yes. With a properly crafted POA and strong documentation, reinstatement is achievable. AMZDOC has successfully reinstated hundreds of accounts after Section 3 suspensions.
