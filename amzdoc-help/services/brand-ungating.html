What Is Brand Ungating on Amazon?

Amazon restricts certain brands to protect consumers from counterfeit products and unauthorized sellers. To sell these restricted brands, you need Amazon's explicit approval — a process known as "ungating" or "brand approval."

The ungating process requires submitting an application through Seller Central with supporting documentation that proves you're an authorized seller of the brand's products. Without proper documentation and the right application strategy, most ungating requests are denied.

Why Brands Get Restricted

  • Brand protection — brands request Amazon restrict their products to prevent unauthorized sellers
  • Counterfeit prevention — Amazon proactively restricts brands with high counterfeit rates
  • Safety requirements — certain product categories require additional certifications
  • Category gating — entire product categories may require approval (Topicals, Grocery, etc.)

Frequently Asked Questions

What is Amazon brand ungating?
The process of getting Amazon's approval to sell restricted brand products. Requires submitting invoices, authorization letters, and meeting brand-specific criteria.
How long does ungating take?
3-14 days depending on the brand. Some have automated instant approval with proper invoices. Manual reviews take 5-7 business days. Complex brands may take 2-3 weeks.
What documents do I need?
Wholesale invoices (10+ units, within 180 days, from authorized distributors), supplier contact info, and sometimes a Letter of Authorization from the brand.
How much does it cost?
Starting at $200 per brand for straightforward approvals, $400-$800 for complex brands. Package deals available for multiple brands.
