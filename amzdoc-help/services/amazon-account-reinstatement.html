Why Amazon Accounts Get Suspended

Amazon suspends seller accounts for a variety of reasons, from policy violations to customer complaints. Understanding the root cause is the first step toward reinstatement. Here are the most common suspension types we handle:

Policy Violations

Selling restricted products, violating listing policies, or failing to meet Amazon's performance metrics (ODR, late shipment rate, pre-fulfillment cancel rate).

Common

Inauthentic Item Complaints

Customer or brand complaints claiming your products are not genuine. Often triggered by unauthorized reselling or missing invoices.

Common

Intellectual Property (IP) Claims

Trademark, copyright, or patent infringement complaints filed by rights owners. Can affect individual listings or the entire account.

Moderate

Counterfeit Complaints

Claims that your products are counterfeit or fake. These are among the most serious and can lead to permanent deactivation if not addressed properly.

Serious

Related Account Violations

Amazon links multiple accounts to the same person or business. Operating multiple accounts without approval leads to suspension of all linked accounts.

Complex

Review Manipulation

Soliciting fake reviews, offering incentives for reviews, or using review services. Amazon's detection systems are increasingly sophisticated.

Serious

Frequently Asked Questions

How long does Amazon account reinstatement take?
Most Amazon account reinstatements take 7-21 days from the initial appeal submission. Simple policy violations may be resolved in as little as 3-5 days, while complex cases involving multiple violations or Section 3 issues can take 30-45 days. Our team works to expedite the process by submitting precise, well-crafted Plans of Action from the first attempt.
How much does Amazon account reinstatement cost?
Our Amazon account reinstatement services range from $500 to $3,000 depending on the complexity of your case. Simple listing suspensions start at $500, standard account deactivations at $1,000-$1,500, and complex cases involving multiple violations or legal issues range from $2,000-$3,000. We offer a free initial consultation to assess your case and provide an exact quote.
Can I appeal an Amazon suspension myself?
Yes, you can appeal an Amazon suspension yourself. However, each failed appeal makes the next one harder. Amazon's Seller Performance team tracks the number of appeals and may escalate to a final review if they receive multiple weak submissions. A professionally crafted Plan of Action significantly increases your chances of reinstatement on the first attempt.
What information do I need to provide?
For a successful reinstatement, you'll need: the original suspension notification from Amazon, your Seller Central account details, any invoices or supply chain documentation related to the flagged products, a clear description of what led to the issue, and the corrective steps you've already taken. Our team will review all documentation and build a comprehensive appeal strategy.
What types of suspensions can you handle?
We handle all types of Amazon seller suspensions including: policy violations, inauthentic item complaints, intellectual property (IP) complaints, counterfeit claims, safety complaints, review manipulation, related account issues, Section 3 violations, and product quality complaints. We also assist with listing-level suspensions and ASIN deactivations.
What happens if the first appeal is denied?
If the first appeal is denied, we analyze Amazon's response to understand what additional information or adjustments are needed. We then revise the Plan of Action and resubmit through appropriate escalation channels. Our service includes follow-up appeals at no additional charge until your account is reinstated or we've exhausted all available channels.
