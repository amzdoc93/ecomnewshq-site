Types of IP Complaints We Resolve

Amazon categorizes intellectual property issues into three main types. Each requires a distinct approach for successful resolution.

Trademark Infringement

Complaints alleging unauthorized use of a registered brand name, logo, or slogan on your product listings. This is the most common type of IP complaint on Amazon. Resolution typically involves proving authorization or negotiating a retraction with the trademark holder.

Copyright Infringement

Claims that your listing uses protected images, text, or creative content without permission. Common when sellers use manufacturer images or copy listing descriptions. We help you source original content or obtain proper licenses.

Patent Infringement

Allegations that your product violates a registered utility or design patent. These are the most complex IP cases and may require legal analysis to determine if the patent claim is valid and enforceable against your specific product.

Why IP Complaints Are Dangerous

Many sellers underestimate the impact of IP complaints. Here's why you should address them immediately:

  • Listing suppression: The flagged ASIN is immediately removed from search results and unavailable for purchase.
  • Account health impact: Each unresolved IP complaint counts as a policy violation on your Account Health Dashboard.
  • Accumulation risk: 3+ unresolved IP complaints can trigger a full account review and potential deactivation.
  • Revenue loss: Suppressed listings generate zero revenue while fixed costs continue to accrue (storage fees, PPC spend, loan payments).
  • Buy Box impact: Even after resolution, your listing may need time to recover its organic ranking and Buy Box share.

Frequently Asked Questions

How do I remove an IP complaint from my Amazon account?
To remove an IP complaint, you need either a retraction from the rights owner or a successful counter-notice submission. AMZDOC helps with both: we contact rights owners to negotiate retractions and prepare legally sound counter-notices when needed. Typical resolution: 5-14 days.
Can an IP complaint lead to account suspension?
Yes. A single IP complaint removes the affected listing, but multiple complaints (typically 3+) can trigger account-level deactivation. Amazon treats repeated IP violations as evidence of systematic policy abuse.
How long does IP complaint removal take?
IP complaint removal typically takes 5-14 days. Retractions from rights owners can resolve in 2-3 days. Counter-notice processes take 10-14 business days. Complex patent cases may take 3-4 weeks.
How much does IP complaint removal cost?
Services start at $300 per complaint for straightforward trademark issues. Complex patent disputes range from $500-$1,500. Package pricing is available for sellers with multiple complaints (up to 5 at a discounted rate).
What if the IP complaint is fraudulent?
Fraudulent IP complaints (sometimes filed by competitors) can be challenged through Amazon's counter-notice process. We help you document the false claim and submit a formal dispute. Amazon takes false IP claims seriously and may penalize the filer.
