Amazon Account Deactivated: What to Do Next

You just received the email every Amazon seller dreads: "Your Amazon seller account has been deactivated." Your listings are gone, your funds are frozen, and orders are cancelled. Panic is natural — but what you do in the next 24-48 hours will determine whether you get your account back.

This guide walks you through exactly what to do, step by step.

Step 1: Don't Panic. Read the Deactivation Notice Carefully.

What to look for in the email:

Amazon's deactivation notice contains critical information. Read it multiple times and identify: (1) the specific policy cited (Section 3, performance metrics, IP violation, etc.), (2) any ASINs mentioned, (3) what Amazon is asking you to provide, and (4) the deadline for appeal (if any).

Do NOT: Submit an appeal within the first 2 hours. Rushed appeals are almost always weak and rejected. Take 24-48 hours to properly investigate, gather documents, and craft a thorough Plan of Action.

Step 2: Identify the Root Cause

Check your Account Health Dashboard in Seller Central (you still have access even when deactivated). Look for:

  • Policy violations — which specific policies were flagged?
  • IP complaints — how many, from which brands?
  • Performance metrics — ODR, late shipment rate, cancellation rate
  • Customer complaints — inauthentic, safety, "not as described"
  • Linked account issues — related account warnings

Step 3: Gather Your Documentation

Before writing your appeal, collect these documents:

  • The original deactivation email (save it, don't lose it)
  • Invoices from your suppliers for all flagged products
  • Letters of authorization from brands (if applicable)
  • Tracking numbers and delivery confirmations for recent orders
  • Any previous correspondence with Amazon about these issues
  • Screenshots of your Account Health Dashboard

Step 4: Write Your Plan of Action

Your POA is the most important document in the reinstatement process. It must address three elements:

  1. Root Cause — What specifically went wrong? Be honest and specific.
  2. Corrective Actions — What have you already done to fix it?
  3. Preventive Measures — What systems have you put in place?

For a detailed guide on writing an effective POA, see our complete Plan of Action guide.

Step 5: Submit Through the Right Channel

Most appeals are submitted through Seller Central > Account Health > Reactivate your account. However, certain suspension types have specific submission channels:

  • Performance-based: Seller Central appeal button
  • IP-related: notice-dispute@amazon.com
  • Verification-required: Seller Central verification portal

Step 6: Wait and Follow Up

After submission, Amazon typically responds within 3-7 days. During this time:

  • Do NOT submit a second appeal while the first is pending
  • Monitor your email (check spam folders too)
  • Prepare additional documentation in case Amazon asks for more information
  • If no response after 7 days, send a polite follow-up through the same channel

What to Do If Your Appeal Is Denied

A denied appeal is not the end. Here's what to do:

  1. Read Amazon's denial carefully — they often hint at what's missing
  2. Strengthen your documentation (better invoices, more specific actions)
  3. Revise your POA to address any gaps Amazon identified
  4. Consider professional help if you've had 2+ rejections
Important: After 2-3 rejected appeals, Amazon may route your case to a senior review team. This is actually a good thing — senior reviewers often have more authority to reinstate accounts. But it also means your next appeal needs to be significantly stronger than the previous ones.

Protect Your Finances During Deactivation

  • Funds hold: Amazon holds your balance for 90 days. You can still receive disbursements for orders shipped before deactivation.
  • FBA inventory: Submit removal orders immediately. Amazon may dispose of inventory if you don't act within 30 days.
  • Subscriptions: Your Professional selling plan fee continues until you downgrade or close the account.
  • Loans: Amazon Lending payments continue regardless of account status.
